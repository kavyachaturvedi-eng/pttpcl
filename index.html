The Foundation Problem

You can't run reliable AI on fragmented industrial data.

Sensor data lives in OSIsoft PI. Maintenance records sit in SAP. P&IDs hide in CAD systems. Engineering documents pile up in SharePoint. Decades of tag renames, sensor swaps, and inconsistent asset numbering across refineries have eroded data quality without anyone noticing.

Until that foundation is fixed, even the best AI agents will hallucinate, drift, and lose engineer trust within weeks. The data model has to come first — and it has to scale.

The Agents

Purpose-built for refining operations.

Every agent has explicit scope, bounded decision authority, and human-in-the-loop design. No autonomous black boxes inside critical operations.

01 · SENTINEL

Continuous anomaly detection

Detects degradation patterns in rotating equipment 60–120 days before failure using ensemble ML tuned to your specific equipment baselines. Surfaces a prioritized daily watchlist ranked by predicted impact.

02 · DIAGNOSTICIAN

Automated root cause analysis

When an anomaly fires, generates a structured RCA brief in under 5 minutes — probable cause, supporting evidence, recommended action, parts needed, MTTR estimate. Every claim cites its data source.

03 · MEMORY

Institutional knowledge capture

Every resolved incident logged with full context. Searchable in Thai or English. Captures the tacit knowledge of senior engineers before retirement — turning tribal expertise into a queryable institutional asset.

04 · OPTIMIZER

Planned maintenance scope intelligence

Analyzes long-term degradation trends and corrosion data to recommend turnaround scope changes. What to inspect, what to defer, what to replace — informed by data, decided by your engineers.
